Technical overview

From responsibility
to working system.

Ahmed developed the ESP32 firmware responsible for medication-dispensing control, stepper-motor operation, DS3231 real-time scheduling, MQTT communication, and medication-schedule storage in flash memory.

He implemented the protocol for receiving schedules, processing dispensing commands, controlling the motors, and verifying pill release through the infrared break-beam sensor. The firmware was organized into modular functions to simplify debugging and future expansion.

He also selected the controller, motors and drivers, RTC, infrared sensors, power supplies, battery and charging system, voltage regulators, and Raspberry Pi power requirements, then developed the initial power-distribution and connection architecture.
